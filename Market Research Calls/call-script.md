# Market Research Call Script

> 30-minute calls. Goal: extract maximum data about their situation, pain, and buying psychology — while naturally positioning yourself as the solution so they're ready to close (or close on the spot).

---

## PRE-CALL (2 min before)

Pull up their profile. Know these before you say a word:

- [ ] Name + what they do (from DMs or Skool bio)
- [ ] Where they are in the pipeline (what stage, what they said in DMs)
- [ ] Anything specific they mentioned (challenge, goal, revenue)
- [ ] Are they a CA member? (skip differently if so)

**Why this matters:** Most people are lazy. Any level of prep looks genius and builds instant trust.

---

## PART 1: OPEN + SET THE FRAME (2 min)

Start casual. You're not a salesman, you're a friend who's genuinely curious.

```
Yo [Name] what's good bro, appreciate you hopping on

So real quick before we dive in — I'm building something new right now
and I wanted to talk to people in my audience who are actually in the
trenches building stuff, not just watching from the sidelines

So this is literally just me asking you questions, learning about your
situation, and seeing if there's anything I can help with. No pitch, no
weird sales thing. Cool?
```

**What this does:**
- Sets the "no pitch" frame (lowers guard completely)
- Positions them as someone special ("in the trenches, not just watching")
- Gets verbal buy-in with "Cool?" — now they're committed to opening up

---

## PART 2: THEIR WORLD (8-10 min)

This is where you spend most of the call. **Their talk time should be 2x yours.** You're mining for gold here — every answer feeds the close later.

### 2A: What they've got going on

```
So tell me about what you got going on right now.
Like what's the business, what are you working with?
```

**Listen for:** Revenue, business model, team, how long they've been at it.

**Follow-ups (pick what's relevant):**

```
How long you been doing that?
```

```
Sick. So what does a typical week look like for you right now?
```

```
Are you doing this full time or is this a side thing still?
```

```
How many clients you working with right now?
```

```
What's revenue looking like monthly? Roughly.
```

**Do NOT move on until you understand their current situation clearly.** This is the "where they are" half of the gap.

---

### 2B: Where they want to go

```
Ok so if we fast forward 90 days and everything goes perfect —
like dream scenario — what does that look like for you?
```

**Listen for:** Specific numbers, lifestyle changes, business milestones. The more specific, the better.

**Follow-ups:**

```
Why 90 days? What's driving the urgency?
```

```
What would hitting that actually change for you day to day?
```

```
On a scale of 1-10 how urgent is solving this for you right now?
```

**Key:** Write down their EXACT words. You'll use these back at them later.

---

### 2C: What's in the way

```
So what do you think is the biggest thing holding you back from that right now?
```

**Listen for:** The real blocker. Usually it's one of: don't know what to do, can't get clients, can't systematize, content isn't working, no accountability.

**Follow-ups:**

```
How long has that been a problem?
```

```
What have you tried so far to fix it?
```

```
How much have you invested in courses or coaching or programs to solve this?
```

```
What worked about those? And what was missing?
```

**This is the money sequence.** Every answer here is ammunition for the close:
- "How long" = cost of inaction (they've been stuck X months)
- "What have you tried" = past solutions failed them
- "How much invested" = they spend money on solutions (budget signal)
- "What was missing" = you position your thing as having what was missing

---

## PART 3: LABEL THE GAP (1-2 min)

Now you reflect it back. This is the Hormozi "Label" move — make the problem explicit so they feel it.

```
Ok so let me make sure I got this right.

You're at [current situation — use their words]. You want to get to
[dream outcome — use their words]. And the main thing in the way is
[blocker — use their words].

You've tried [what they tried] and it helped with [what worked] but
it was missing [what was missing].

That sound about right?
```

**Wait for them to say yes.** This is a micro-commitment. They just agreed that they have a problem, they haven't solved it, and past attempts fell short.

---

## PART 4: BRIDGE TO YOUR THING (3-4 min)

**Do NOT pitch features.** Sell the vacation, not the plane ride. Keep this under 3 minutes.

```
Yeah so honestly that's exactly why I'm building this.

The way I see it, most people who are trying to [grow on IG / scale
their business / get clients through content] are missing one of
three things:

1. A clear system — like an actual step-by-step roadmap for YOUR
   specific situation, not some generic course

2. Real feedback loops — someone who actually looks at your content,
   your DMs, your offer and tells you what to fix. Not just "post
   more" lol

3. Accountability with other people who are actually doing it — not
   a dead community, like real people in the trenches with you

Most programs give you maybe one of those. Maybe two. But if you're
missing any of the three it falls apart.

That's what I'm building. A 90-day program where you get all three
dialed in specifically for your situation.
```

**Then immediately ask:**

```
Does that make sense? Like does that line up with what you feel
like you've been missing?
```

**If they say yes** — you're in the red zone. Move to Part 5.

**If they push back** — ask what specifically doesn't resonate. Don't argue, just listen. Their pushback = more data.

---

## PART 5: THE SOFT CLOSE (3-5 min)

You're not hard closing. You're checking if they want in. The energy is "I think I can help you, do you want me to?"

```
So here's what I'm thinking.

Based on what you told me — [reference their specific situation] —
I'm pretty confident I could help you get to [their dream outcome]
in 90 days.

Would you be interested in being one of the first people in this?
```

### If they say YES or show interest:

```
Sick. So let me break down how it works real quick.

It's a 90-day program. You get [the 3 pillars — system, feedback,
accountability]. We start with a 1-on-1 onboarding call where I
build out your specific roadmap, then we've got weekly group calls
plus direct access to me.

Investment is $4K for the 90 days.
```

**Then shut up.** Let them process. Don't fill the silence.

### If they ask about price:

```
Yeah so it's $4K for the full 90 days.

The way I look at it — if we get you to [their stated goal],
that's [X] per month in revenue. So you'd make that back in
[timeframe]. Does that math make sense to you?
```

### If they want a guarantee:

```
Here's how I think about it honestly.

I can't guarantee results because I can't do the work for you.
But what I can guarantee is the system works — I've used it myself
and with my clients. And I'm going to be in your corner the whole
90 days making sure you're executing.

If you show up, do the work, and it doesn't move the needle —
I'll keep working with you until it does. That fair?
```

---

## PART 6: HANDLE OBJECTIONS

### "Let me think about it"

```
Totally get it. Serious question though — what specifically are
you going to be thinking about?

Like is it the money, the timing, or something about the program
itself?
```

**This surfaces the REAL objection.** "Let me think about it" is never the real reason.

---

### "I need to talk to my [spouse/partner]"

```
For sure. What do you think their main concern would be?
```

Then:

```
And what would make them say absolute yes?
```

**You're now handling the real objection through their partner's mouth.**

---

### "I can't afford it right now"

```
I hear you. Can I ask — is it that you don't have the money, or
that you're not sure it's worth the investment?
```

If it's truly no money:

```
Respect bro. Tell you what — stay in the Skool group, keep
grinding, and when the timing is right hit me up. I'll make
sure there's a spot for you.
```

If it's uncertainty about ROI — go back to the math. Use their numbers.

---

### "I'm already in another program"

```
Oh nice what program? ... How's that going for you?
```

**Listen.** If it's working, genuinely tell them to stick with it. If it's not:

```
So you're paying for [X] and it's not getting you [their goal].

What would need to be different about a program for it to actually
work for you?
```

---

### "I need to see results / case studies first"

```
That's fair. What kind of results would you need to see to feel
confident? Like what would someone need to have accomplished that
would make you say "ok yeah this is legit"?
```

**Write down exactly what they say.** This is literally them telling you what social proof to build.

---

## PART 7: CLOSE OR PLANT THE SEED (2 min)

### If they're ready:

```
Sick dude let's do it. I'll send you the link to get started
right after this call. Welcome aboard bro
```

### If they're warm but not ready:

```
No pressure at all bro. Here's what I'd say — I'm launching
April 3rd, first cohort is capped at [X] spots.

Take the time you need, and if you want in just hit me up before
then. I'll save you a spot.
```

### If they're not a fit:

```
Honestly bro I appreciate you being real with me. It sounds like
[reason] so this probably isn't the right time.

Keep grinding though, you're on the right track. And if anything
changes hit me up.
```

---

## ALWAYS END WITH: DATA EXTRACTION

No matter what happens with the close, grab this data before hanging up. Frame it as "helping me build this better."

```
Before we hop off — can I ask you like 3 quick questions?
This stuff genuinely helps me build this thing better.
```

### The 5 data questions:

1. **"What would make this a no-brainer for you?"**
   → Reveals their ideal offer structure

2. **"What's the #1 thing you'd want out of a program like this?"**
   → Reveals what to lead with in marketing

3. **"If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about your business tomorrow, what would it be?"**
   → Reveals deepest pain point (often different from what they said earlier)

4. **"What's been your experience with online programs or coaching before? Like good and bad."**
   → Reveals trust barriers and what to avoid

5. **"If I launched this at $4K, what would need to be true for you to say yes instantly?"**
   → Literally tells you how to close them later

## POST-CALL CHECKLIST

- [ ] Write down their 3 biggest pain points (in their words)
- [ ] Write down their dream outcome (in their words)
- [ ] Note their objections and what would flip them
- [ ] Rate them: Ready to close / Warm / Not a fit
- [ ] If warm: set a follow-up date and what you need to show them

## CHEAT SHEET: ENERGY & TONE REMINDERS

| Moment | Energy |
|--------|--------|
| Opening | Warm, casual, "what's good bro" |
| Their story | Genuinely curious, reactive ("damn that's solid") |
| Their pain | Empathetic, slower pace, let them talk |
| Label the gap | Calm, clear, mirror their words back |
| The bridge | Confident but not salesy, "here's what I'm building" |
| The ask | Direct but pressure-free, "would you be interested?" |
| Objections | Curious not defensive, "what specifically?" |
| Close | Hype, "sick dude let's go" |
| Exit | Respectful, door open, "keep grinding bro" |

---

## KEY PRINCIPLES (TAPE THESE TO YOUR MONITOR)

1. **Pain is the pitch.** The more they talk about their struggle, the more motivated they are to solve it. Don't rush past the pain.

2. **Use their words, not yours.** When you reflect their exact language back, they feel deeply understood. This builds trust faster than any pitch.

3. **Sell the vacation.** Never explain the curriculum, the modules, the Slack channel. Talk about where they'll be in 90 days.

4. **Shut up after the ask.** The first person who talks after the price drop loses. Let them process.

5. **One reframe max.** If they say no twice, respect it. Leave the door open and move on. Pushing past that kills the relationship.

6. **The close starts in the DMs.** Everything they told you before this call is ammo. Reference it. Show you were listening.

7. **If they shouldn't buy, tell them.** Nothing builds more credibility than "honestly bro I don't think this is the right fit for you right now." They'll come back later or refer someone who is.
