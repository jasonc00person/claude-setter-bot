# Sales Call Script & Framework

> Built from 12 market research calls (Mar 4-10) + Hormozi CLOSER framework.
> 5/14 closed. All 3 closes used: urgency + existing relationship + flexible pricing + hands-on framing.
> Zero people paid $4K sticker. This script fixes that.

---

## Pre-Call (5 Min Before)

**Do this every single time. 5 min of prep = looks like genius-level preparation.**

1. **Re-read the setter notes / DM thread.** Know their name, niche, revenue, what they said in DMs
2. **Identify their pain.** What problem drove them to book?
3. **Pick your angle.** Which of the 3 pillars matters most to them? (content system, monetization, accountability)
4. **Know their objection before they say it.** Budget? Proof? Already burned? Time?
5. **Energy check.** Stand up, do 10 jumping jacks, whatever. You cannot close in a low-energy state

**Repeat back their situation in the first 60 seconds.** Never make them re-explain. This alone separates you from every other coach they've talked to.

## The Call Structure (30 Min)

| Phase | Time | Purpose |
|-------|------|---------|
| 1. Connect & Clarify | 2-3 min | Build rapport, remind them why they're here |
| 2. Dream Outcome | 3-4 min | Get them to paint the picture |
| 3. Pain Cycle | 8-10 min | This is where you win or lose. 90% of the sale happens here |
| 4. Bridge (3-Pillar Pitch) | 2-3 min | Quick, not a lecture |
| 5. Offer + Guarantee | 2-3 min | Stack value, present price, remove risk |
| 6. Close or Handle Objections | 5-8 min | Ask for the sale. Handle whatever comes up |
| 7. Lock It In | 2-3 min | Payment + onboarding. BAMFAM |

**Golden ratio: They talk 70%, you talk 30%.** If you're talking more than them, you're pitching, not selling.

---

## Phase 1: Connect & Clarify (2-3 min)

**Goal:** Build instant rapport. Remind them of the action they took. Set the frame.

### Open with energy + their name
```
yooo [Name] what's good bro, appreciate u hopping on
```

### Reflect back what you already know (FROM SETTER NOTES)
```
so I saw in our chat u mentioned [specific thing from DMs - their niche, their challenge, what they're working on]
tell me more about that, what's the situation rn?
```

**Why this works:** Hormozi says repeating their situation back is "exceptionally positive" -- it shows you listened and immediately builds trust. Every other coach makes them start from scratch.

### Set the frame (casual, not corporate)
```
cool cool, well here's how this works -- super chill, just a conversation
I just wanna learn about what u got going on, see if I can help, and if not no worries at all
sound good?
```

**NEVER say:** "This is a market research call" or "I'm not pitching you." That killed urgency on 8/12 calls. You ARE here to help them and potentially work together. Own that.

---

## Phase 2: Dream Outcome (3-4 min)

**Goal:** Get them to verbalize their ideal future in vivid detail. The bigger the gap between where they are and where they want to be, the stronger the close.

### The question
```
so paint me a picture bro -- if everything went perfectly over the next 90 days, what does that look like for u?
```

### Follow-ups to deepen it
```
what does that change for u day to day?
```
```
and what does that mean for [their specific situation - quitting their job, moving out, supporting family]?
```

**What you're listening for:**
- Specific dollar amounts (not vague "more money")
- Emotional drivers (freedom, proving themselves, family, quitting job)
- Urgency signals: "I'm done with..." / "I need to..." / "I can't keep..."

**If their dream is too small** (like Brayden's "$1K/month"):
```
ok so $1K is the first milestone -- but real talk, where do u actually wanna be? like if we're talking 6-12 months out what's the real number?
```

**Write down their exact words.** You're going to use their language back to them in the close.

---

## Phase 3: Pain Cycle (8-10 min)

**This is where you win the sale. Hormozi: "The pain is the pitch." Spend 90% of time here, 10% on your product.**

### Question 1: The challenge
```
so what's been the biggest thing holding u back from getting there?
```

Then shut up. Let them talk. Follow up with:
```
how long has that been going on?
```

### Question 2: What they've tried
```
what have u tried so far to fix that?
```

**Listen for:** courses, coaches, programs, free content, doing it alone. Each failed attempt = ammunition.

### Question 3: The cost of those failures
```
and how much have u invested in all that so far? like time and money total
```

Then:
```
and where are u at now after all that?
```

**This is the kill shot.** They're about to realize they've spent thousands of dollars and months/years and they're still in the same spot. You don't need to say it -- they'll feel it.

### Question 4: The cost of inaction (Hormozi: quantify the daily cost)
```
so real talk -- if nothing changes and u keep doing what you're doing rn, where are u in 6 months?
```

Or more direct:
```
what's it costing u every month to NOT have this figured out?
```

### Question 5: Urgency check
```
on a scale of 1-10 how urgent is this for u to solve rn?
```

**If 7+:** You're in business. Move to Phase 4.

**If under 7:**
```
interesting -- why not higher?
```
Then:
```
and why isn't it a 1?
```

The second question makes them sell themselves on why this matters. (Hormozi 1-to-10 close)

### What you're building during this phase:
A mental stack of THEIR pain points in THEIR words that you'll reflect back in the pitch. You are not selling your program. You are showing them the cost of staying where they are.

**TRAP TO AVOID:** Don't rush this to get to your pitch. On calls where you spent 10+ min in pain (George, Saharat), you closed. On calls where you rushed through (Victoria, Pierre), you didn't.

---

## Phase 4: Bridge / 3-Pillar Pitch (2-3 min)

**Goal:** Connect their pain to your solution in under 2 minutes. Three pillars, that's it.

### The transition
```
ok so let me make sure I got this right --

u want [their dream outcome in their exact words]
the main thing holding u back is [their #1 challenge in their words]
u've tried [what they tried] and spent [what they spent] and u're still at [where they are]

does that sound about right?
```

Wait for the yes. Then:

### The 3-pillar pitch

```
so here's the thing -- most people in your situation are missing one of three things

1. a content system that actually works -- not just posting randomly and hoping, but a repeatable process that brings in leads every week

2. a monetization path -- knowing exactly what to sell, how to price it, and how to close it through DMs and calls

3. accountability -- someone in the trenches with u making sure u actually do the work and don't fall off

which of those hits hardest for u?
```

**Customize the 3 pillars based on what they told you in Phase 3.** The above is the default for beginner creators. For agency owners (Pablo-type), swap to: systems/SOPs, AI automation, scaling without burning out.

### Then position yourself as the fractional SMM (NOT a coach)
```
so basically what I do is I come in as like a fractional social media manager for ur brand

I'm not just teaching u stuff and hoping u figure it out -- I'm literally in the trenches with u every week, reviewing ur content, writing ur scripts, setting up ur systems, doing 1-on-1 calls

think of it less like a coaching program and more like hiring someone who's obsessed with making ur page blow up
```

**Why "fractional SMM" works:** It closed 3/3 deals. "Coaching program" closed 0/9. People don't want another course. They want someone doing it WITH them.

---

## Phase 5: Offer + Guarantee (2-3 min)

**Goal:** Stack value, present the price anchored against what they've already spent, and remove all risk with the guarantee.

### Value stack (rapid fire, don't dwell on each one)
```
so here's what this looks like --

- weekly 1-on-1 calls with me, just u and me going over everything
- I review ur content before u post, scripts, hooks, thumbnails, all of it
- full content system setup -- ur schedule, ur batching process, ur editing workflow
- monetization roadmap -- we build ur offer, ur DM scripts, ur funnel together
- group calls 4x a week with other creators at ur level for extra support
- direct access to me on WhatsApp -- u can hit me up anytime between calls
```

### Anchor the price against their sunk cost
```
so u said u've already spent like $[their number] on [courses/coaches/programs] and u're still at [where they are]

this is $3K for 90 days of me being ur dedicated growth partner
```

**Pricing rules (from what actually closed):**
- Default: $3K (payment plan: $1K x 3 months)
- Existing CA/community members: credit what they've paid. Gobb paid $2,500 before, so he owed $500
- Pre-revenue leads with high urgency: $500 upfront + $500 on first client (pay-on-results)
- NEVER discount. Change TERMS (payment plans, pay-on-results), not price

### The guarantee (this closed all 3 deals)
```
and here's my guarantee -- if u don't land at least 3 high-ticket clients in 90 days, I keep working with u for free until u do

no BS clauses, no hidden stuff, no "well u didn't do the homework so it doesn't count"

I literally don't stop until u win
```

### Then shut up. Let it land.

Don't add more details. Don't keep selling. Hormozi: "If they say yes, shut up and close."

---

## Phase 6: The Close (5-8 min)

### Ask for the sale (simple, direct)
```
so what do u think bro, u wanna do this?
```

### If YES:
```
sick dude let's go
```
Jump to Phase 7. Do NOT add more details or sell yourself out of the sale.

### If they hesitate, use the decision framework:

**Step 1: Identify the real objection**
```
totally fair bro -- what's the main thing holding u back?
```

**Step 2: Handle it (see Objection Cheat Sheet below)**

**Step 3: Re-close**
```
so if we can solve [that concern], are u in?
```

### If they need to "think about it":
```
for sure bro -- what specifically are u thinking about? like is it the money, the timing, or something else?

cuz I'd rather just talk through it now so u can make the best decision either way
```

This surfaces the real objection. "Think about it" is never the real reason.

### The nuclear close (use sparingly, only when they're 7+ on urgency but stalling):
```
real talk bro -- do u think the reason u're on this call today is because u've struggled to pull the trigger on decisions like this in the past?

cuz every person I've worked with who got results had that same moment where they were nervous and did it anyway

the question is -- is the guy u are right now gonna get u to the guy u wanna be?
```

---

## Phase 7: Lock It In (2-3 min)

**BAMFAM: Book A Meeting From A Meeting. Never end a call without the next step locked.**

### Collect payment
```
cool let me send u the payment link rn
```
Send link while on the call. Watch them pay. Do not hang up until payment is confirmed.

### Book onboarding
```
sick dude, so first thing -- let's get ur onboarding call booked

when works for u this week? I wanna get u started immediately
```

### Set expectations
```
alright so here's what happens next:
1. I'm gonna send u the backbone worksheet -- fill that out before our first call
2. we'll hop on the onboarding call [day/time] and map out ur full 90-day plan
3. after that we're locked in -- weekly calls, content reviews, the whole thing
4. I'll add u to the WhatsApp group with the other creators too

any questions?
```

### End with energy
```
bro I'm genuinely hyped for this, ur gonna crush it. talk soon
```

## PRICE / MONEY

### "That's too expensive" / "$3K is a lot"
```
totally get it bro -- and honestly most people feel that way at first

but real talk, u said u've already spent $[their sunk cost] on [their failed attempts] and u're still at [same spot]

so the question isn't really whether $3K is a lot -- it's whether u can afford to keep spending money on stuff that doesn't work

what if we did $1K now and $1K a month? does that feel more doable?
```

**Hormozi principle:** Nothing is "too expensive" -- it's just not worth it yet. Rebuild the value, don't cut the price.

### "I don't have the money right now"
```
I hear u bro -- what does ur situation look like? cuz I've got some flexibility on how we structure this

[If they have SOME money]: what if u did $500 now and the rest over 3 months?
[If truly $0]: what if u just get started and pay me when u close ur first client? I just need to know u're serious
```

**Never say "that's ok, maybe later."** Explore creative terms first. If truly impossible, funnel to low-ticket.

### "I can find this info for free on YouTube"
```
for sure u can bro -- and u should totally use free content

but real talk, how long have u been watching free content? and where are u at?

the information isn't the problem -- it's the implementation, the accountability, and having someone customize it to YOUR situation

that's what u're paying for
```

### "Other programs are cheaper"
```
yea for sure -- and which one are u looking at?

[let them answer]

ok so what would that get u specifically?

[let them answer]

and if that program was the same as mine, which would u choose?

[they'll tell you why yours is better]

exactly bro -- so the question is whether that difference is worth [price gap] to u
```

## TRUST / PROOF

### "Do you have case studies / results?"
```
yea so I've got [Name] who's in month [X] rn and already [specific result]

and honestly the reason I can guarantee 3 clients in 90 days is because I've already done it for myself -- went from $0 to $10K/mo in [timeframe] using the exact system I'm gonna build for u

but here's the real thing -- a case study is someone else's result. what matters is YOUR situation. and based on what u told me about [their niche + pain], I'm confident I can get u there
```

### "I got burned by another coach / program before"
```
bro I feel that, I've been there too -- I spent $30K on programs before I figured it out

and honestly most of them WERE trash -- just courses with group calls where u never get direct help

that's exactly why this is different:
- it's me and u 1-on-1 every week, not a course u watch alone
- I review ur actual content, not generic advice
- and if I don't get u results in 90 days, I keep working with u for free

I literally have zero incentive to not get u results
```

**The Miggle refund story (trust nuke -- use when they're skeptical):**
```
I've had to refund people before too -- and I'll do it
I'm not gonna be like "well u didn't show up to this call so no refund"
```
> Ayden's response: "that's why I took the call today. I genuinely feel like you care about people." This story alone can close skeptical leads who've been burned. The willingness to refund = proof you're not like the other coaches.

### "How do I know this will work for MY niche?"
```
great question -- so what's ur niche exactly?

[let them explain]

ok so here's the thing -- the content system and monetization framework works across niches because it's about the fundamentals: hooks, retention, offers, and DM sales

the specifics of YOUR niche is what we customize together on our 1-on-1 calls

that's the whole point -- it's not a cookie-cutter course, I'm literally building this WITH u
```

```

here's the thing -- u're never NOT gonna be busy. and if u can't build this when u're busy, what happens when life gets even crazier?

the system I'm building with u is designed to save u time -- batching, templates, automation. u'll actually have MORE time after the first few weeks

```
what changes after [that event]?

[let them answer]

[usually yes]

so the question is whether waiting is actually helping u or just delaying the thing u already know u need to do

## DIRECTION / "WRONG VEHICLE"

### "I want to build a SaaS, not run a service business"
> Closed Ayden at $3K PIF with this exact sequence.

```
totally get it bro -- and honestly u should keep building that on the side

but here's the thing -- ur vehicle is the agency. the SaaS is just how u deliver
ur clients don't care if it's ur software or ManyChat or Claude -- they're paying for the result

so what if we just got u to 10K a month first?
then u can decide whether to go full SaaS or keep scaling the service
either way u'll have more money and more clarity to make that call
```

**Key techniques:**
1. **Don't fight the dream.** Validate it, then redirect
2. **"Play it out"** — ask "what do you think will happen if you run meta ads to a landing page at $150/mo?" Let them realize the math doesn't work
3. **"Handle what's in front of us"** — collapse scattered decisions into one immediate goal
4. **Anti-SaaS positioning (2026):** "Building a SaaS right now is a red ocean. Everyone's vibe coding. Claude keeps wiping out SaaS products." Use sparingly, only when prospect is wavering

### "I don't want a service business, it's just a job"
```
I hear u -- but real talk, whether it's SaaS, agency, or coaching, u're never gonna escape fulfillment

the difference is -- at 10K a month from services, u have cash and skills to build whatever u want next

right now tho, u're making [their current revenue] and going in circles trying to pick the right vehicle

what if we just locked in on what's already working?
```

## STALLS / "LET ME THINK ABOUT IT"

### "I need to think about it"
```
for sure -- what specifically are u thinking about?

like is it the price, the time commitment, whether it'll work, or something else?
```

Then handle whatever they surface. "Think about it" is never the real objection.

### If they won't tell you what's really going on:
```
totally fair bro -- let me ask u this

what would make this a hell yes for u?

[they tell you]

ok and what would make this a definite no?

[they tell you -- and usually realize their "no" reasons are solvable]

it sounds like u're not saying no, u're just nervous about making the decision -- and honestly that's exactly how every person I've worked with felt right before they said yes
```

### "Let me sleep on it / I'll let you know tomorrow"
```
for sure bro -- and hey real talk, I'm not gonna pressure u

but just from experience, when someone says "let me sleep on it" they usually already know the answer

so what's ur gut telling u rn?
```

# CALL DEBRIEF TEMPLATE

After every call, answer these 5 questions:

1. **Did I spend enough time in pain?** (Target: 8-10 min)
2. **Did I let THEM talk more than me?** (Target: 70/30)
3. **Did I ask for the sale directly?** (Not "so what do u think" but "u wanna do this?")
4. **Did I collect payment on the call?** (Verbal yes without payment = not closed)
5. **What's the one thing I'd do differently?**
