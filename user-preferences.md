# User Preferences & Operating Rules

> Rules and preferences learned from experience. For the full DM playbook, stage rules, and voice guidelines, see `CLAUDE.md`.

## Session Workflow Preferences (Priority Order)

1. **Blue dots (unread replies) ALWAYS come first** ‚Äî Before sending ANY new follow-ups or openers, scroll through the ENTIRE inbox (both Primary AND General) to find ALL blue dots. Handle every single reply before moving to fresh outreach. Replies > new openers.
2. **Work hot leads in pipeline** ‚Äî Stage 5, 4, 3, then work down
3. **Find high-quality ICP leads first** ‚Äî When batch-opening new leads, PRIORITIZE by ICP fit:
   - **Tier 1 (open first):** Western/American/Caucasian names + good profile pic (clear photo, looks like a real creator). Examples: Reece Spence, Robert Daoud, Damien Fraser.
   - **Tier 2 (good enough to outreach):** Other English-speaking leads with real creator profiles.
   - **Tier 3:** Everyone else. Skip non-English speakers entirely.
   - Look for accounts that already received a lead magnet (ManyChat auto-delivered). Quality > quantity.
4. **Requests tab** ‚Äî Last resort. Only when all quality leads are worked and you're just waiting on replies.
- Don't over-focus on General inbox ManyChat flood ‚Äî most aren't worth pursuing
- **Every 10 interactions** (message sent, lead checked, info gained ‚Äî any step counts), update pipeline-state.md
- **Keep ManyChat tags updated immediately** ‚Äî Every time a lead changes stage, update their ManyChat Contact Tag right away (not at checkpoints). Tags: `In Conversation`, `Qualified`, `Booking Link Sent`, `Call Booked`, `Unqualified`, `follow up`. This keeps the inbox organized and lets Jason see lead status at a glance.
- Jason may jump in and respond to friends mid-session ‚Äî don't touch those convos
- **Use ManyChat Contacts tab to search for people** ‚Äî Inbox search is unreliable (doesn't always find contacts). Use the Contacts tab instead for reliable lookup by name or handle.
- **Always verify the chat header name before typing** ‚Äî After clicking a contact in the sidebar, confirm the chat panel header matches the intended recipient before typing anything. The panel doesn't always switch reliably.

## Mistakes to Avoid (Learned from Experience)

- **NEVER use em dashes in DMs!** Dead giveaway it's AI. Use commas, periods, or split into separate messages.
- **Don't overuse the fire emoji.** It's predictable and feels automated. Vary your reactions ‚Äî use other emojis, "that's sick", "love that", "honestly that's dope" etc. Mix it up every time. Sound human, not like a bot with a script.
- **Acknowledge first, then ALWAYS send AV** ‚Äî When a lead replies to an opener, acknowledge first ("thats sick bro..."), then send the AV question as a follow-up message. Don't skip the AV. Two separate messages, not bundled, but both get sent.
- **Never leave a convo on a statement** ‚Äî Every message we send should either ask a question or prompt a reply. Don't leave a lead hanging on just an acknowledgment. Keep the conversation moving until they're booked.
- Accidentally sent "Lmaoo ikr" to Melissa (friend) ‚Äî never interact with friend conversations
- Tried to send nudge to Eshaan ‚Äî user corrected: no double texting ever
- Called Danele Louw "bro" when she's clearly a woman ‚Äî always check gender first
- **When a lead sends THEIR calendar link instead of using ours, play it off with humor first.** Don't just go "heres my link tho" ‚Äî that's too generic and robotic. Example: "no way u just tried to calendar mog me haha" then "but seriously here's my link, let me know if you find a time that works!" THEN send Calendly. Match their energy, make it fun.
