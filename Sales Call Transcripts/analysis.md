## Top Objections (Ranked by Frequency)

1. **Price / Budget** (9/12 calls) — $4K is the #1 killer. Every non-close cited money as a barrier.
2. **Proof / Case Studies** (5/12) — Ayden, Pablo, Salim, Pierre, CFong all wanted evidence Jason has done this for people like them.
3. **Cohort vs. 1-on-1** (4/12) — Ayden, Pablo, Pierre, CFong prefer customized over group.
4. **Program not ready yet** (3/12) — April 3 launch date means prospects are buying a promise, not a product.
5. **Already burned by other coaches** (3/12) — Saharat, Salim, Damien all had bad prior experiences.

---

## What Consistently Worked

- **Market research reframe** — Lowered resistance on almost every call. Prospects opened up more.
- **Personal story (dropout, $0 to 10K/mo)** — Landed with younger/earlier-stage leads (Brayden, Damien, Saharat, Victoria).
- **Curiosity-first discovery** — Asking about their business before pitching. Every call that went well used this.
- **Flexible pricing/payment plans** — All 3 closes used creative payment structures (free entry, $1K split, $3K payment plan). The $4K sticker price closed zero people.
- **Done-for-you elements** (scriptwriting, video review, 1-on-1s) — George and Gobb both lit up when Jason offered hands-on work, not just coaching.
- **Guarantee with no BS clauses** — "3 clients in 90 days or I keep working with you free" was the cleanest version.
- **Fractional SMM positioning** — Reframing from "coach" to "fractional social media manager" resonated with action-oriented leads.

---

## What Consistently Didn't Work

- **$4K sticker price** — 0/12 paid full price. Every close was discounted or restructured.
- **Cohort pitch to advanced leads** — CFong, Pablo, Ayden all rejected group format immediately.
- **No case studies / social proof** — The biggest gap. Multiple leads asked for proof and Jason couldn't provide it.
- **Pitching before program exists** — April 3 launch date made several leads say "hit me up when it's ready."
- **Generic outcome promises** — "10K/month in 90 days" got pushback from anyone already above 5K.

---

## Lead Qualification Patterns

**Closes had in common:**
- High urgency (quitting jobs, returning from breaks, "done with side quests")
- Existing relationship with Jason (prior client or community member)
- Accepted creative payment terms
- Wanted hands-on help, not a course

**Non-closes had in common:**
- Budget constraints (even when interested)
- Wanted proof before committing
- Preferred self-paced or 1-on-1 over cohort
- Lower urgency ("I can wait 2-3 months")

---

## Key Insights

1. **Drop the $4K anchor on first calls.** Every close used a lower/split price. Lead with $3K or payment plans.
2. **George + Saharat + Gobb are your first 3 case studies.** Document their results aggressively for April 3.
3. **Build an agency-specific track** — Pablo and Ayden are qualified, have budget, but need proof you can help agencies/setters specifically.
4. **"Fractional SMM" positioning > "coaching program"** — The hands-on framing closed deals. The course framing didn't.
5. **The April 3 deadline is a double-edged sword** — Creates urgency but also gives people permission to wait.
