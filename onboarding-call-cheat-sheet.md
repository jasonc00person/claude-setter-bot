# Onboarding Call Cheat Sheet / Agenda

**Duration:** 45-60 minutes
**Format:** 1-on-1 Zoom, screen share, Fathom recording on
**Pre-req:** Client has completed the Backbone Worksheet before the call

---

## Before the Call (5 min prep)

- [ ] Pull up client's Backbone Worksheet
- [ ] Review their sales call transcript/notes (what was their main pain? What closed them?)
- [ ] Have the 90-day roadmap ready to screen share
- [ ] Have the scaling tracker ready to show
- [ ] Have the Skool/WhatsApp group ready to add them

---

## Call Structure

### 1. Vibe Check (2-3 min)
*Build rapport, set the tone — casual, bro energy*

- "What's good bro, how we been?"
- "Anything crazy going on before we dive in?"
- Quick personal check-in — acknowledge where they're at emotionally (excited? nervous? overwhelmed?)

**Goal:** Make them feel comfortable and heard. This sets the tone for the entire coaching relationship.

---

### 2. Backbone Review (15-20 min)
*Screen share their worksheet. Go section by section.*

**Vision Section:**
- Review current problems, desired situation, desired income
- Confirm: "Is this still accurate or has anything changed since we talked?"
- Make sure the "why" is emotional and real — this is what keeps them going when it gets hard

**Niche / ICP Section:**
- Who exactly are they serving?
- Get SPECIFIC: age range, income level, platform, what stage of business
- "If you had to describe your perfect client in one sentence, who is it?"
- Red flag check: is their ICP too broad? Too niche? Can they actually REACH these people?

**Offer Section — THIS IS THE MOST IMPORTANT PART:**
- What's the outcome? Make it **Boolean** — either they hit it or they don't
  - Bad: "I'll help you grow your brand" (vague)
  - Good: "I'll help you hit 10K followers" or "land your first paying client" or "hit $10K/mo" (measurable, true/false)
- What's the price? Is it appropriate for their stage?
  - First clients: 50% of full price is fine. Get results first, raise price later.
- What's the unique mechanism? Why would someone go with THEM over a competitor?
  - "What makes your approach different from every other person doing this?"
  - Help them name it — give it a branded name (e.g., "The Artist Backbone Worksheet", "The Musician Playbook")
- What are the deliverables? Structure them chronologically:
  - Month 1: they get X
  - Month 2: they get Y
  - Month 3: they get Z
  - Client should be able to see how the deliverables lead to the result
- What's the guarantee?
  - Keep it clean, no stipulations
  - "[Outcome] in 90 days or I keep working with you for free"
  - Our clients were burned by conditional guarantees — we don't do that

**Edit the worksheet LIVE on the call.** Don't just review — actually fix things together. This makes the call feel high-value and collaborative.

---

### 3. 90-Day Roadmap Walkthrough (10-15 min)
*Screen share the customized roadmap*

- Walk through each phase: what happens in month 1, 2, 3
- Emphasize: "You don't have to figure anything out. Just check the boxes."
- Show them it's customized to THEIR situation (reference specifics from their backbone/sales call)
- Point out: "You can go faster than this. If it says week 3 and you finish early, just move on."
- Make it clear: the roadmap is the GPS. They follow it, they get the result.

**Key phrases:**
- "As long as we check off all the boxes and take it step by step, we will get to [outcome] by month 3"
- "This is specifically for YOUR situation — not a generic thing"
- "You just need to turn off your brain and follow the steps"

---

### 4. Content Plan (5-7 min)
*Set up their content engine*

- **Film day:** Pick a day. Block the calendar. No calls, no nothing — just filming.
  - Ideation + scripting: day before film day
  - Film day: batch everything
- **Volume:** Start with 3 reels + 1 YouTube per week (adjust based on their capacity)
- **Content OS:** Do they have a tracker? Notion calendar view is ideal.
  - "Can you share it with me so I can see your posting consistency?"
  - If they don't have one, share a template
- **Trial reels are dead.** Everything goes on the main grid.
- **ManyChat:** Is it set up? Connected? Active? If not, that's a Week 1-2 task.
- **Lead magnet:** Do they have one? If not, plan one (YouTube video, PDF, free community)

---

### 5. Accountability Setup (3-5 min)

- **Weekly 1-on-1 calls:** Pick a recurring day/time. Book it now.
  - Best if it's BEFORE their film day so you can review scripts
  - Or AFTER so you can review what they posted
- **Inner circle calls:** 3x/week, optional but encouraged. Show them the calendar.
- **WhatsApp group:** Add them NOW on the call
  - "Every day, I'm gonna check in — what did you get done today? I expect you to post what you did."
  - "One day off is an error. Two days in a row is a habit. We don't want bad habits."
- **Content submissions:** Send scripts/videos to Jason before posting for feedback (at least in the beginning)
- **Scaling tracker:** Show them the sheet. They fill it out weekly: outreach volume, calls booked, calls taken, close rate, new customers.

---

### 6. Set Up DM Setting Conversations (3-5 min)
*Get the pipeline started*

- Do they have a setting script? If not, priority #1 is documenting one.
  - "Get it in a Google Doc. This is what we'll use later for the AI setter."
- Start warm outreach: DM followers, ManyChat inbounds, anyone who's engaged with content
- Frame it as market research to start: "I'm not trying to sell you. I just want to hop on and see if I can help."
  - But if they're qualified on the call → book a sales call from the call (meeting from a meeting)
- "The most important thing right now is just getting the pipeline started. We need calls on the calendar."

---

### 7. Quick Claude/AI Overview (3-5 min, OPTIONAL)
*Only if there's time and they're interested — don't overwhelm*

- Show them Claude desktop app + Cowork
- Explain: choose a folder = gives Claude context/knowledge
- "Try getting it to open the browser and do something simple — order food, navigate a website"
- We'll do the full AI DM setter setup on a future call once they actually have lead flow to work with

---

### 8. Action Items + Close (3-5 min)
*Make sure they leave crystal clear*

- Ask THEM to repeat back what they're doing this week:
  - "What are you going to do before our next call?"
  - They should name: content, outreach, ManyChat, setting convos, backbone refinements
- Book the next 1-on-1 call RIGHT NOW on the call
- Send them:
  - [ ] Fathom recording link
  - [ ] Backbone worksheet (if they don't have it yet)
  - [ ] 90-day roadmap
  - [ ] Scaling tracker
  - [ ] WhatsApp group invite (if not added yet)
  - [ ] Content OS template (if needed)

**Close with energy:**
- "Do you feel clear? Do you know what you need to do?"
- "If you need anything — text me on WhatsApp, post in the group, DM me. I got you."
- "Let's get it."

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Don't overwhelm them.** Focus on what they need to do THIS WEEK. The roadmap handles the rest.
2. **Don't let them spiral into strategy debates.** If they start "but what about SaaS vs agency" — redirect: "Let's just take care of what's in front of us first."
3. **Don't skip the backbone review.** Even if they think it's done, there's ALWAYS something to tighten (vague outcome, too-broad ICP, unnamed unique mechanism).
4. **Don't skip booking the next call.** If you end without the next call booked, momentum dies.
5. **Don't spend too much time on AI/Claude setup.** Save it for a later call. They need content and conversations first, not automation.

---

## Post-Call Checklist

- [ ] Send Fathom recording via WhatsApp or Zoom chat
- [ ] Finalize their 90-day roadmap (make any edits discussed on the call)
- [ ] Add them to WhatsApp group (if not done on call)
- [ ] Share any resources mentioned (templates, trackers, etc.)
- [ ] Update pipeline / client tracker with their status
- [ ] Set a reminder to check in on WhatsApp tomorrow

---

## The Onboarding Call in One Sentence

**Review their backbone, show them the roadmap, set up accountability, give them this week's action items, book the next call, and send them off fired up.**
